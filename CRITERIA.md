# CTS Criteria

*What counts. What doesn't. Why.*

---

## Protocol Protection Rules

These are the hard boundaries.  
If any of the following appear — it is no longer #CTS.

```
NEVER ALLOWED:
  × Official fee to participate
  × Official certificate or license
  × "CTS token" or any on-chain gate
  × Paid access tier
  × Central governing body
  × Ranking of "best" participants
  × Any single entity controlling the standard

IF SOMEONE PROPOSES:          THE ANSWER IS:
"Let's make a paid badge"  →  That is not #CTS.
"Let's make a foundation"  →  That is not #CTS.
"Let's rank the top givers" → That is not #CTS.
"Let's require approval"   →  That is not #CTS.
```

The protocol belongs to the community.  
One person can start it.  
No one can own it.

V1 must be simple enough to copy without permission.  
Because if V1 is clean — it spreads without you.  
That is the goal.

---

## The Core Requirement

A CTS action is a verifiable, recurring, public give-back.

Three words. All three matter.

**Verifiable** — proof exists and can be checked.  
**Recurring** — it happens consistently, not once for attention.  
**Public** — it's posted with `#CTS @yourname`.

---

## Who It's For

**Creators and influencers earning from audience:**
- Course sales
- Signal or trading groups
- Premium content subscriptions
- Mentoring or coaching fees
- Sponsorships and brand deals
- Any revenue where the audience is the engine

**Anyone else:**
- 1% of personal income, OR
- A fixed monthly amount — €20, €50, whatever is real for you

If you've ever been on the poor side,  
you know what €20 can do.  
It can feed a family for days,  
buy medicine,  
or keep the lights on.

---

## The Amount

**Option A:** 1% of net monthly income  
**Option B:** 10% of a specific revenue stream  
**Option C:** Any fixed amount you commit to monthly

The amount is never required to be disclosed publicly.  
The act and the rhythm must be.

---

## Privacy and Proof

You can split your monthly donation into multiple smaller ones.  
The last confirmation of the month sets your window date for ImpactJar.

You can say you give 1% of net income  
without disclosing what that income is.

The rhythm is the proof. Not the number.

---

## What Counts as Proof

- Screenshot of donation confirmation
- Date clearly visible
- Cause or recipient visible
- Posted publicly on social media with `#CTS @yourname`

Optional but encouraged: tag the cause or organization directly.

---

## Where to Donate

Anywhere the money reaches real people  
and the impact can be seen or verified:

- GoFundMe, JustGiving
- Foundation from your home country
- Local hospital campaign
- Food bank
- Refugee aid
- Homeless kitchen
- Disaster relief
- Any legitimate registered charity

**Not eligible:**
- Donations to yourself or your own projects
- Payments disguised as donations
- Causes where fund flow is unverifiable

Community can spot fakes fast.

---

## What Does NOT Count

- Free content, tutorials, or educational posts
- Unpaid work or volunteering alone (without financial give-back)
- Discounts or giveaways
- One-time donations without recurring commitment
- Vague pledges without proof

Teaching what a pot is  
has never been the same  
as sharing what's in it.

---

## The Recurring Requirement

CTS is not a one-time action.  
It's a monthly rhythm.

A single donation proves nothing about character.  
Twelve consecutive months proves consistency.  
Consistency is what changes norms.

See: `CHANGELOG.md` for a live example of what this looks like.

**Influencers who already give privately:**

Whatever is real for your situation.  
Start. Keep going.
