*Transparent. Public. Permanent.*

*Because verifiable means visible. Even our own rules.*

## Why This Document Exists

People don't give because they fear scams.

Hacked foundations. Internal fraud. Fake campaigns.
These are real. The fear is valid.

ImpactJar's answer:

You donate **directly to the charity's own website.**
ImpactJar never holds your money.
The screenshot proves the money moved.
The charity receives it directly.
No middleman. No jar holding funds.

The verified directory exists to reduce the research burden —
not to replace your judgment.

Every organization in the directory has been checked
against the criteria below.
Every check is dated.
Every removal is documented.

### TIER 2 — STANDARD VERIFICATION

Any registered charity not on Tier 1 lists.

**All five criteria must be met:**

```
CRITERION 1 — Legal registration
Registered nonprofit status in country of origin.
Examples:
  Poland:  KRS registration
  UK:      Charity Commission registration
  USA:     501(c)(3) IRS status
  Germany: eingetragener Verein (e.V.)
  EU:      Equivalent national registration

CRITERION 2 — Direct donation path
URL must go directly to charity's own donation page.
Money flows: donor → charity directly.
Not via ImpactJar. Not via individual.
Not via unverified payment processor.

CRITERION 3 — Public financial reporting
At least one annual report or equivalent
published within the last 24 months.
Must be publicly accessible.
Shows: where money goes, what it does.

CRITERION 4 — Visible impact
Website must show what donations actually fund.
Not just mission statements.
Specific: lives helped, projects funded,
outcomes measured.

CRITERION 5 — Active status
Last donation received within 12 months.
No open fraud investigations from credible sources.
Organization responds to contact within 30 days.
```

**Review time:** 5–7 days  
**Reviewer:** mr_Finch_X (manual until #5,000 entries)  
**After #5,000:** Community panel of 3 verified members + mr_Finch_X

---

### TIER 3 — COMMUNITY SUBMISSIONS

Organizations submitted by ImpactJar members via bullet spend.

**Process:**
```
1. Member spends 1 bullet to submit
2. Organization enters review queue
3. Reviewed against Tier 2 criteria (5–7 days)
4. If approved: added to directory, bullet consumed
5. If rejected: bullet refunded + reason given publicly
6. Submitter notified either way
```

**Bullet refund triggers:**
- Organization doesn't meet Tier 2 criteria
- Duplicate — already in directory
- Unverifiable within 7 days — refunded, can resubmit later

## Annual Re-verification

Every organization in the directory is checked annually.

**Tier 1:** Re-verified when source lists update (automatic).

**Tier 2 and Tier 3:** Annual check every 12 months from addition date.

**What gets checked:**
```
□ Organization still active?
□ Donation page still working?
□ Any fraud reports in last 12 months?
□ Annual report updated?
□ Legal registration still valid?
```

**Outcomes:**
```
All checks pass:
→ "Verified [year]" badge updates
→ Remains in active pool

One or more checks fail:
→ Status changes to "UNDER REVIEW"
→ Community notified
→ Organization has 30 days to resolve
→ If resolved: reinstated
→ If unresolved after 30 days: removed from active pool
```

## Removal Policy

**Immediate removal triggers:**
```
- Active fraud investigation from credible source
- Donation page down for 7+ days with no response
- Organization dissolved or defunct
- Credible evidence of fund misuse
- Request from the organization itself
```

**Important — permanent record rule:**

When an organization is removed —
**it stays in the record with a status note.**

```
Example:
[Organization name]
Added: March 2026
Removed: August 2026
Reason: Fraud investigation opened by [authority]
Status: INACTIVE — not eligible for new donations
```

ImpactJar entries that donated to this organization
**keep their timestamps and proof.**

The giving happened.
That is permanent.
Only future routing is affected.

## What We Don't Verify

ImpactJar is not an auditor.
We are not responsible for:

- How organizations spend every donation
- Organizational effectiveness beyond basic criteria
- Internal governance decisions
- Political or religious positions of verified organizations

For deep charity research, we recommend:
```
GiveWell (givewell.org)
Giving What We Can (givingwhatwecan.org)
Founders Pledge (founderspledge.com)
Charity Navigator (charitynavigator.org)
```

## The Honest Paragraph

Many people don't give because they've seen the headlines.
Hacked foundations. Internal fraud. Fake campaigns.

This directory doesn't eliminate that risk.
No list does.

What it does:
- Removes the research burden for common choices
- Ensures basic legal legitimacy
- Gives you a starting point, not a guarantee

The proof system — screenshot, date, public post —
is your receipt. Not ours.

The charity receives your money directly.
ImpactJar records that it happened.
The risk between those two facts
is managed by choosing verified organizations
and using direct donation pages.

That's the honest version.
