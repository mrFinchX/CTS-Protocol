# ImpactJar — Metrics

*What gets measured. What doesn't. Why it matters.*

---

## The Core Principle

Vanity metrics measure extraction.  
ImpactJar metrics measure contribution.

These are opposite directions.

---

## What ImpactJar Tracks

### 1. Give-Back Actions
- Number of logged donations
- Causes supported (category, not amount required)
- Consecutive months active
- First action date (the timestamp)

### 2. Rhythm Score
- Consistency of monthly window (±5 days)
- Streak length in months
- Grace periods used (transparency, not punishment)

### 3. Collective Impact
- Total members in record
- Retention rate (% still active)
- Total causes supported across community
- Geographic spread of impact

### 4. Network Effect
- Referral tree depth
- Members brought in and still active
- Member state: Signal → Beacon → Legend

---

## What ImpactJar Does NOT Track

- Exact donation amounts (privacy — never required)
- Follower counts
- Engagement rates
- Revenue figures
- Social media reach

None of these measure what you put back.  
None of these belong here.

---

## The Retention Rate

The single most important number ImpactJar produces.

```
Total joined: [n]
Still here: [n]
Retention: [%]
```

This is the signal that travels.  
A 97% retention rate means:  
people who start this almost never stop.

That's the statistic that changes minds.  
Not the headcount.

---

## The Streak

Consecutive months of maintained rhythm.

Not the longest streak.  
Not the highest amount.  
Just: are you still going?

**Why streaks matter:**

Most people who start helping  
never stop.

The streak makes that visible.  
A 14-month streak is a 14-month argument  
that this works.

---

## The Timestamp

Every member's first logged action is permanent.

Not editable. Not deletable. Not transferable.

This is the founding record.  
Long after ImpactJar scales beyond 10,000 —  
the record will show who was here  
when it was still fragile.

---

## The Collective Number

Total real-world impact is calculated  
at each 1,000-member milestone.

Not as a fundraising total —  
as a proof-of-concept document.

Published publicly at 5,000 members.  
Named after the people who got it there.

---

## Why These Metrics and Not Others

Because the question ImpactJar asks is:

*What did you actually put back?*

Not:  
- How many people saw you?
- How much did you grow?
- How loud were you?

The current metrics ecosystem  
answers the wrong questions  
very precisely.

ImpactJar answers the right question  
imperfectly but honestly.

And honest imperfection  
beats precise misdirection  
every time.

---

## Future Metrics (Post-Automation)

After 10,000 members and system automation:

- On-chain verification integration
- Cross-platform give-back aggregation
- Agent-readable trust score (give-back history as data standard)
- ImpactJar score embedded in creator profiles

The long game:  
an entity's give-back history  
becomes part of how AI agents evaluate trust.

Not just for influencers.  
For anyone who builds a public record.
